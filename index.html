To the man that inspired me to be the person I am today.My father.

My father, my hero

My father has always been a central figure in my life. He thought me how to be a good person and what are the values of the family. When I was a child I always considered my father as a hero and I had in plan to find a husband exactly like him. Naturally, the relationship over the years changed and took a dangerous turn during my adolescence. I remember staying days or even weeks without talking to each other. Growing up and looking back at those moment I feel sorry to have treated him that way, and only now I realise how we are alike and what a good person he is. He gave me precious insights that made me the person I am today. For this reason, I would like to thank him for all he did for me.

Thank you for...teaching me to respect other people

One of the most important lesson I have learned from my father is how to respect the different opinions of other people and do not take it personally if someone does not agree with me on something.

Thank you for... bringing me to beautiful places of the world

Together we have seen many places around the world, from the pyramids in Egypt to the mosque in Instanbul. From all the journeys we had I have wonderful memories that will stay with me forever. My father loves to travel and discover new cultures and places. You can notice his passion when, during a trip, he takes his lonely planet and starts reading out loud the description of the places.

Thank you for... teaching me that reading books is important

As a child I had never liked reading much, but growing up my father taught me that reading was really important for keeping my brain active, discover new vocabularies and learning from people smarter than me. Only after high school I understood what he meant and I started reading more.

Thank you for...teaching me how to be more confident with myself

Me and my father are quite alike. When he was a child he was very shy and, like me, he often felt uncomfortable to talk to other people. Growing up he built up his confidence and seeing me going through the same struggles, he kindly shared with me few gold advises.

Thank you for...supporting me, whatever decision I make

When, after my solo adventure in Ireland, I decided to quit college and move with my boyfriend to Dublin my father was hearthbroken. Despite the fact that he knew he would see me less often, he did not try to stop me or convinced to stay. He simply told me how sad he was to see me going but at the same time I could count on his full support.

Deborah Rimei
